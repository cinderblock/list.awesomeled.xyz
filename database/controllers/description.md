Controllers are the brains of your LED installation.
They receive data from pattern software (via protocols like Art-Net, sACN/E1.31, or DDP) and convert it into the precise timing signals that addressable LEDs need.

When choosing a controller, consider:

- **Output count** - How many separate LED strips/strands you need to drive
- **Pixel capacity** - Total number of LEDs across all outputs
- **Input protocols** - Art-Net, sACN, DDP, or direct USB/WiFi control
- **Standalone capability** - Can it play patterns without a connected computer?
- **WLED compatibility** - Open-source firmware support for DIY flexibility
